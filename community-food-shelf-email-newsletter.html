COMMUNITY SHELF

Email Newsletter

OVERVIEW

This email newsletter was designed and built collaboratively by Amy McGowan and Andrea Martz. The project was completed in response to an assignment during Cohort 4 of Moms Can Code School (https://www.momscan.co/) to create a single issue of an email newsletter.

Our newsletter was designed for a fictional community food pantry. The purpose of the newsletter is to engage community members and show them the ways they can make a difference for their neighbors in need.

This page tells the story of how our project came to life.

THEME, AUDIENCE AND PURPOSE

Theme

Amy and I are both concerned about social justice issues. We had several ideas for potential themes, but we both liked the idea of creating a community newsletter for a nonprofit, and we settled on doing it for a fictional local food shelf.

Photo by Nathan Lemon on Unsplash

Audience and Purpose

The target audience is residents in the geographic area served by the food shelf who have connected in some way with it in the past and shared their email address. These people include current and past donors of food or money as well as current and past volunteers.

The purpose of the newsletter is to engage community members by showing them the ways they can make a difference for their neighbors in need and inspiring them to contribute.

SELECTING THE CONTENT

Main Content

We decided that the main content would include short, inspirational descriptions of ways to get involved and a call-to-action (CTA) button. Andrea drew inspiration from her communityâ€™s food pantry VEAP (Volunteers Enlisted to Assist People) in Bloomington, MN and proposed a list of possible ways that community members could help a fictional food shelf.

We narrowed the list to five ways to help:

  • Being a driver for the van service
  • Attending a gala fundraising event
  • Guiding clients through the grocery shopping area
  • Participating in or starting a community garden
  • Bringing food to those in need through the Mobile Food Pantry

Image by Tumisu from Pixabay

Secondary Content

We also wanted to include other resources, such as some engaging and easy-to-consume educational video links about the hidden prevalence of hunger and food insecurity. In addition, we wanted to provide a way to easily locate a food pantry in another community in case the user were to move or just want to help friends or relatives get involved in their own communities.
